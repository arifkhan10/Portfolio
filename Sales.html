Global Trade Analysis Dashboard

  • Category: Tableau Dashboard
  • Client: Personal
  • Project date: 18 May, 2024
  • Project URL: Global Trade Analysis Dashboard

Summary

  • Total Trade Value Map Display: Shows selected country's trade value based on trade flow (export, import, re-export, re-import). Note: USA has no re-import activity. Australia stopped re-export activity since 1999.

  • Market Size 1988-2016: USA: Largest exporter, importer, and re-exporter. Canada: Largest re-importer. USA never re-imported commodities.

  • Top Categories Based on Quantity Data: 12 categories with commodities, analyzed for trade value in billions of USD. Insight: Rankings of categories based on demand can be observed by selecting country and trade flow. For example, selecting Australia and Export will show top categories.

  • Trade Balance Observation: Calculated as total export value minus total import value. Positive balance = Trade Surplus; Negative balance = Trade Deficit. 1988-2016: Three countries had a positive trade balance. Insight: Ranking (1988-2016): Australia > USA > Canada. Ranking (2012-2016 and 2016 alone): Australia > Canada > USA. Trade Surplus countries may levy higher import duties to promote domestic consumption.

  • Top Commodities Based on Quantity Observation: Charts show top commodities based on traded quantity for selected trade flow. Insight: Example: Selecting Canada and Export (1988-2016) shows Wheat (except durum wheat) as the largest exported commodity. Similar observations can be made for other countries and trade flows.

  • Unit Price of the Commodity Over the Years Observation: Unit price = Trade Value / Quantity. Chart shows unit prices for commodities over a period. Insight: Information on unit prices can be obtained for selected country, trade flow, and commodity. Forecasting techniques can predict future unit prices.

  • Demand for the Commodity Over the Years Observation: Chart shows demand for commodities over a period. Unit prices and total trade value (USD in billions) are included. Insight: Fluctuations in the chart indicate changes in quantity traded. Information on unit prices for selected country, trade flow, and commodity is available. Overall, the analysis provides comprehensive insights into global trade dynamics, highlighting key trends, market sizes, trade balances, top commodities, and unit price variations over time.
