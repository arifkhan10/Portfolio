E-Commerce Product Delivery Prediction

  • Category: Machine Learning
  • Client: Personal
  • Project date: 01 April, 2023

Summary

  • Key Factors Impacting Delivery: The analysis revealed that product weight (2500-3500 grams) and cost (less than $250) significantly affect on-time delivery rates. Additionally, products shipped from warehouse F, likely due to its proximity to a seaport, show a higher rate of timely delivery.

  • Customer Behavior Insights: Frequent customer calls correlate with delayed deliveries, while customers with more prior purchases experience more timely deliveries, indicating a strong relationship between purchase history and delivery reliability. Furthermore, products with over 10% discount tend to be delivered on time more often than those with 0-10% discount.

  • Model Performance: Among the machine learning models used, the decision tree classifier achieved the highest accuracy of 69%. Other models like the random forest classifier, logistic regression, and K Nearest Neighbors followed with accuracies of 68%, 67%, and 65%, respectively.

  • Implications for Strategy: To improve delivery performance, the company should focus on optimizing the shipment process for products that weigh 2500-3500 grams and cost under $250, consider enhancing logistics for warehouse F, and leverage insights from customer behavior, such as incentivizing repeat customers and managing customer communication more effectively.
